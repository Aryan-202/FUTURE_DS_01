## 🔍 About the Task
In this real-world internship task, I have worked with business sales data to perform the kind of analysis that data analysts do for real companies, startups, and agencies. 

The goal is not just to create charts, but to help a business answer important questions such as:
- Which products generate the most revenue?
- How do sales change over time?
- Which categories or regions are most profitable?
- Where should the business focus to grow faster?

## ✅ What I Did
During this task, I performed the following:
- **Data Cleaning & Preparation:** Cleaned and organized raw sales data (Excel/CSV format) to ensure accuracy.
- **Core Analysis:** Analyzed revenue trends over time, identified top-selling products, and pinpointed high-value categories/regions.
- **Visualization:** Built a professional, client-ready dashboard for interactive data exploration.
- **Insights & Strategy:** Wrote clear insights and actionable recommendations as if advising a real business.

## 📈 Key Insights & Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis conducted in the [Jupyter Notebook](notebooks/analysis.ipynb) and visualized in the [Dashboard](dashboard/FUTURE_DS_01.pbix):

1. **Revenue Trends:** There is a consistent upward trend in revenue from 2014 to 2017, with 2017 showing the highest total sales ($733k+). Peak sales generally occur in the **Q4 holiday season**.
2. **Product Performance:** **Phones** and **Chairs** are the top revenue generators, contributing significantly to the overall sales.
3. **Regional Strength:** The **West Region** is the strongest market, followed by the East. The **South** shows significant room for targeted marketing growth.
4. **Customer Segments:** The **Consumer Segment** represents the largest portion of sales and profit, suggesting a core loyal base.
5. **Profit Optimization:** While sales are high in categories like **Tables**, high discounts often lead to negative profits. Recommending a revision of the discounting strategy for these items.
